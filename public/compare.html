Mission Trip Software Comparison:
Finding the Right Fit for Your Church

There are a handful of mission trip management software platforms available today. Each one takes a different approach. Here is a straightforward look at what they offer, where they differ, and how to decide which one fits your church best.

What to Look for in Mission Trip Software

Before comparing platforms, it helps to know which features actually matter day-to-day. These are the criteria we hear trip leaders ask about most.

Online Donation Processing

Can donors give online with a card or bank transfer? What are the processing fees, and who pays them?

Fundraising Tracking

Can each team member see their individual progress? Can leaders see the full picture for the entire team at a glance?

Task and Document Management

Can you assign tasks (passport copies, medical forms, deposits) and track who has completed what?

E-Signatures for Waivers

Can participants sign liability waivers and medical releases digitally? What about minors who need a guardian co-signature?

Budget and Expense Tracking

Can you build a trip budget, log expenses against it, and see where you stand financially at any point?

Team Communication

Is there a built-in way to reach your team, or are you still copying email addresses into BCC fields?

Reporting and Reconciliation

Can your finance team pull clean reports? Can you reconcile donations against bank deposits without a spreadsheet?

Setup Time and Price

How long before your team can actually use the platform? And what does it cost — upfront, monthly, or per transaction?

Where GO Stands Out

A few areas where GO takes a meaningfully different approach.

A Free Tier That Actually Works

Zero Platform Fees

GO never charges a platform fee on top of payment processing. The only fees are standard Stripe rates: 2.9% + $0.30 for cards, 0.8% (capped at $5) for ACH. Every dollar possible goes to the mission.

Built-in Discipleship

Most mission trip software stops at logistics. GO connects to a full discipleship platform with pre-trip reading plans, devotionals, and structured courses. Trip preparation becomes spiritual preparation, not just packing lists.

Penny-Accurate Donation Splitting

When a donor gives to your trip, the software splits the amount across your team with zero rounding loss. Remainder pennies are distributed using a fair algorithm. Your finance team never has to chase missing cents.

Where Others May Be a Better Fit

No platform is the right answer for everyone. Here are situations where another tool might serve you better.

When to consider ServiceReef

  • ServiceReef has deep integrations with missions agencies and sending organizations. If your church works closely with an agency that already uses ServiceReef, staying in that ecosystem can simplify coordination and avoid duplicate data entry.
  • Their network-level features are designed for organizations that manage relationships across multiple agencies and field partners over many years.

When to consider Managed Missions

  • Managed Missions has a longer track record with large organizations and denominations. If your church is part of a network that has already standardized on Managed Missions, the institutional knowledge and existing workflows may outweigh feature differences.
  • Migration always has a cost. If your team has years of historical data in another platform and is comfortable with the current workflow, switching may not be worth the disruption.
