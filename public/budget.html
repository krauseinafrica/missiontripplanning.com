Mission Trip Budget Guide:
What It Actually Costs

Realistic numbers, line-by-line breakdowns, and the hidden costs most trip leaders learn about the hard way. Everything you need to build a budget your church can trust.

Average Mission Trip Costs by Destination (2026)

The single biggest factor in mission trip cost is where you are going. Airfare alone accounts for 40-50% of most international trip budgets, and it varies enormously by destination. Here is what churches are actually paying per person in 2026, including flights, lodging, meals, insurance, and basic ministry supplies.

Domestic (United States)

$500 - $1,500

Driving distance trips come in under $500. Flights within the US push costs higher, but lodging at churches or camp facilities keeps the total manageable. Most common for first-time trip churches.

Central America / Caribbean

$1,500 - $2,500

Guatemala, Honduras, Dominican Republic, Haiti. Shorter flights from the US keep airfare around $400-700. In-country costs are low, but trip insurance and group logistics add up quickly.

South America

$2,000 - $3,500

Peru, Ecuador, Brazil, Colombia. Longer flights ($600-1,200) and more complex ground transportation. Visa requirements vary by country and can add $50-160 per person.

Africa

$2,500 - $4,500

Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, South Africa, Zambia. Flights are $900-1,800 and often require layovers. Vaccinations (yellow fever, malaria prophylaxis) add $200-400 per person that many teams underestimate.

Asia

$3,000 - $5,000

Philippines, Thailand, India, Nepal, Cambodia. The longest flights mean the highest airfare ($1,000-2,000). Time zone adjustment often requires an extra day on each end, adding to lodging costs.

Europe

$2,500 - $4,000

Eastern Europe and the Balkans are more common for church trips. Airfare is moderate ($600-1,200), but Western European lodging and meals are significantly higher than developing countries.

These ranges reflect all-in per-person costs for a 7-10 day trip with a team of 10-20 people. Smaller teams pay more per person. Costs based on 2025-2026 church mission trip data.

Budget Line Items Every Trip Should Include

The difference between a well-planned trip and a financial headache comes down to what you account for upfront. These are the budget categories every mission trip leader should build into their plan from day one.

Category Typical % of Budget Notes
Airfare 40-50% Book 4-6 months out. Group rates through travel agents can save 10-15%. Watch bag fees.
Lodging 15-20% Mission compounds, guest houses, or partner churches. Hotels raise this significantly.
Meals 8-12% Often included with lodging at mission facilities. Budget $15-25/day internationally, $25-40 domestic.
Ground transportation 5-8% Airport transfers, daily transport to work sites. Chartering a van or bus is usually cheaper than daily rides.
Travel insurance 3-5% Non-negotiable. Expect $50-150 per person for trip cancellation + medical evacuation coverage.
Passport and visa fees 1-3% US passport: $165 (new) or $130 (renewal). Visa fees vary from $0 to $160+ depending on country.
Vaccinations and medications 2-4% Yellow fever, typhoid, malaria pills, hepatitis A. Check CDC requirements 3+ months out. Some insurance covers these.
Ministry supplies and materials 3-5% VBS materials, construction supplies, medical supplies. Often cheaper to purchase in-country.
Communication 1-2% International SIM cards, portable Wi-Fi hotspot, or phone plan add-ons. Budget $20-50 per person.
Team shirts and supplies 1-2% Matching shirts, lanyards, luggage tags, first aid kits. Small per-person cost but adds up with large teams.
Emergency buffer 10-15% Medical emergencies, flight changes, unexpected costs. This is not optional. Unused funds can offset the next trip.

Fundraising Math: Setting the Right Per-Person Goal

Getting the per-person fundraising target right is critical. Set it too low and you are covering the gap out of the church budget. Set it too high and participants get discouraged. Here is the formula experienced trip leaders use.

Example: 15-Person Trip to Guatemala

Airfare (15 x $550) $8,250
Lodging and meals (15 x $600) $9,000
Ground transportation $2,400
Travel insurance (15 x $85) $1,275
Ministry supplies $1,500
Communication and misc $750
Team shirts and supplies $375
Subtotal $23,550
Emergency buffer (12%) $2,826
Total Trip Budget $26,376
Per-Person Fundraising Goal $1,759

A few adjustments most leaders make from here:

  • Round up to the nearest $50 or $100. In this case, $1,800 per person gives you a small additional cushion.
  • Scholarship spots. If you are covering 2 participants who cannot fundraise, divide their share across the remaining 13. That brings the goal to about $2,030 per person.
  • Church subsidy. Many churches cover a flat amount per person ($200-500) from the missions budget, reducing individual goals.
  • Early bird discount. If participants commit and pay a deposit 6+ months out, you can lock in lower airfare and pass the savings through.

The key is transparency. When participants see exactly where their money goes, they fundraise with more confidence, and their donors give more generously.

Hidden Costs Most Trip Leaders Miss

These are the line items that do not show up in your initial budget but show up on your credit card statement. Experienced leaders build these in from the start.

Checked Bag Fees

$35-75 per bag, each direction. A team of 15 with one checked bag each can spend $1,050-2,250 on baggage alone. Budget for at least one checked bag per person round trip.

Airport Meals and Layovers

A 4-hour layover with 15 people buying airport food is easily $200-300. International itineraries with long layovers can double this. Pack snacks and set a per-meal limit.

Currency Exchange Fees

ATM fees abroad run $3-5 per withdrawal plus 1-3% foreign transaction fees. Multiplied across a team making multiple withdrawals, this adds $100-300. Bring some cash exchanged before departure.

Tips and Gratuities

Drivers, translators, cooks, and local hosts. Tipping culture varies by country, but budget $5-15 per team member per day for group tips to local staff and partners.

Post-Trip Expenses

Thank-you gifts for donors, printed photo books for the team, a celebration dinner, and report materials for the church. Budget $200-500 for the group.

Travel Insurance Gaps

Basic travel insurance often excludes adventure activities, pre-existing conditions, and pandemic-related cancellations. Read the fine print. Upgraded coverage adds $25-50 per person.
